Health Tips

Limit sugary drinks

Sugary drinks like sodas, fruit juices, and sweetened teas are the primary source of added sugar

Avoid ultra-processed foods

Ultra-processed foods are highly palatable, meaning they are easily overeaten, and activate reward-related regions in the brain, which can lead to excess calorie consumption and weight gain

Don’t fear coffee

Despite some controversy over it, coffee is loaded with health benefits. It’s rich in antioxidants, and some studies have linked coffee intake to longevity and a reduced risk of type 2 diabetes, Parkinson’s and Alzheimer’s diseases, and numerous other illnesses

Get enough sleep

The importance of getting enough quality sleep cannot be overstated. Poor sleep can drive insulin resistance, can disrupt your appetite hormones, and reduce your physical and mental performance

Stay hydrated

Drinking water is the best way to stay hydrated, as it’s free of calories, sugar, and additives.

Eat plenty of fruits and vegetables

Vegetables and fruits are loaded with prebiotic fiber, vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants, many of which have potent health effects.

Get moving

Doing aerobic exercise, or cardio, is one of the best things you can do for your mental and physical health.

Don't do drugs, smoke and drink alcohol

Smoking, harmful use of drugs, and alcohol abuse can all seriously negatively affect your health.

Avoid artificial trans fats

Artificial trans fats are harmful, man-made fats that are strongly linked to inflammation and heart disease
